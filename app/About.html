Hello, this is the about section which I have yet to write. It's a work in progress, folks.

This application was made with R's shiny and networkD3 packages and the D3.js JavaScript library.

A bank holding company is simply a firm that controls one or more banks. I've made an attempt here of visualizing the structures of such firms, with an eye toward portraying the scale and complexity of organization. (No analysis yet.) The underlying data come from the National Information Center (NIC) website, where holding company data can be queried via the Institution Search form. While the NIC reports data for U.S. companies only, many of them control a large number of international subsidiaries based in Europe, Asia, and elsewhere.

The company structures are not strict hierarchies, in the sense that subsidiaries may be partially controlled by multiple parent companies (this is not uncommon). The definition of "control" (say, of company B by company A) is established in the instructions for the FR Y-10 Reporting Form and includes such criteria as

  • Ownership of 25% or more of any class of B's voting securities;
  • Election of a majority of B's board of directors, trustees, general partners, or other high-level management positions;
  • Ownership of "all or substantially all" of B's assets;
  • ...

The forms also provide information on entity types and locations. (What does the "location" represent? Place of incorporation?) Some holding companies control large numbers of domestic and international non-bank subsidiaries.
